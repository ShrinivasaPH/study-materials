📊 Z-Tests (Large Samples)
Z-Test: One Sample
Parametric • Known σ
Compare sample mean against population mean when σ is KNOWN and n > 30
Z-Test: Two Samples
Parametric • Independent
Compare means of two independent groups when both σ known and n₁,n₂ > 30
Z-Test: Difference of Means
Parametric • Large Sample
Test if difference between two means equals a specific value (large samples)
T-Test: One Sample
Parametric • Most Common
Compare a sample mean against a hypothesized population mean when σ is UNKNOWN
T-Test: Independent
Parametric • Two Groups
Compare means of TWO INDEPENDENT groups (different subjects)
T-Test: Paired
Parametric • Before-After
Compare means when observations are PAIRED (same subjects measured twice)
⚖️ ANOVA - Comparing 3+ Groups
One-Way ANOVA
Parametric • F-Test
Compare means across 3 or MORE INDEPENDENT GROUPS in one factor
Kruskal-Wallis
Non-Parametric • Robust
Non-parametric alternative to ANOVA for NON-NORMAL or ORDINAL data
🎯 Proportions & Categorical Data
Z-Test: Proportion
Parametric • Binary
Test if sample proportion equals hypothesized proportion
Chi-Squared GOF
Non-Parametric • Categories
Test if observed frequencies match expected distribution
Chi-Squared Independence
Non-Parametric • Contingency
Test if two categorical variables are independent
🔗 Correlation & Association
Pearson Correlation
Parametric • Linear
Test LINEAR relationship between TWO CONTINUOUS VARIABLES
Spearman Correlation
Non-Parametric • Rank
Test MONOTONIC relationships - works with non-normal ordinal data
🎲 Non-Parametric Tests
Mann-Whitney U
Non-Parametric • 2 Groups
Alternative to independent t-test for non-normal data
Wilcoxon Signed-Rank
Non-Parametric • Paired
Alternative to paired t-test for non-normal paired data
Fisher's Exact Test
Exact • Small Samples
Exact test for 2×2 tables when chi-squared assumptions fail
✓ Assumption Checking
Shapiro-Wilk Test
Assumption Check
Check if data is approximately normally distributed
Levene's Test
Variance Check
Check if groups have equal variances
